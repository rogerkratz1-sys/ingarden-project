SUPPLEMENT S3 — Motif Stability and Deterministic Clustering for a Grimm Narrative: “How Some Children Played at Slaughtering”

Overview
This supplement documents the canonical Grimm robustness run (seed?42) and provides the reproducible artifacts, verification metrics, motif cardinalities, and representative exemplars used in the main paper. We generated 5,000 peripheral permutations of the Grimm narrative, embedded them using the sentencetransformer model described in the Methods section, and applied the K?Ward perturbation pipeline with K = 6 and n_runs = 500.

Verification used the canonical embeddings and canonical clustermembership output (seed?42). The canonical run yields a stable, reproducible sixway partition that maps cleanly onto the sixmotif taxonomy used in the manuscript. The silhouette value is near zero, indicating soft motif boundaries in embedding space; motifs therefore represent structural tendencies rather than sharply separated geometric clusters.

Data and poset extraction
Source text: “How Some Children Played at Slaughtering” (Grimm)
In a city named Franecker, located in West Friesland, some young boys and girls between the ages of five and six happened to be playing with one another. They chose one boy to play a butcher, another boy was to be a cook, and a third boy was to be a pig. Then they selected one girl to be a cook and another girl to be her assistant.  The assistant was to catch the blood of the pig in a little bowl so they could make sausages.  As agreed, the butcher now fell upon the little boy playing the pig, threw him to the ground, and slit his throat open with a knife, while the assistant cook caught the blood in her little bowl.

A councilman was walking nearby and saw this wretched act.  He immediately took the butcher boy with him and led him into the house of the mayor, who instantly summoned the entire council.  They deliberated about this incident and didn’t know what to do with the boy, for they realized it had all been part of a children’s game.  One of the councilmen, a wise old man, advised the chief judge to take a beautiful red apple in one hand and a Rhenish gold coin in the other.  Then he was to call the boy and stretch out his hands to him.  If the boy took the apple, he was to be set free.  If he took the gold coin, he was to be killed.  The judge took the wise man’s advice, and the boy grabbed the apple with a laugh.  Thus he was set free without any punishment.

Source: How Some Children Played at Slaughtering (Brothers Grimm), as presented in The Original Folk and Fairy Tales of the Brothers Grimm, trans. Jack Zipes; Princeton Univ. Press. Parsed events (Chambers & Jurafsky style)
We extracted 12 compact, verbcentered events from the above Grimm tale. Each event is a short predicate with its core arguments (agent, patient, instrument/location when relevant).
1. E1 — choose_roles: children choose roles (butcher, cook, pig, cook assistant).
2. E2 — assign_assistant_task: assistant assigned to catch pig’s blood in a bowl.
3. E3 — butcher_attacks_pig: butcher falls on boy playing pig and slits his throat with a knife.
4. E4 — assistant_catches_blood: assistant catches the blood in her bowl.
5. E5 — councilman_sees: a councilman walking nearby sees the act.
6. E6 — councilman_takes_butcher: councilman takes the butcher boy to the mayor’s house.
7. E7 — mayor_summons_council: mayor summons the entire council.
8. E8 — council_deliberates: council deliberates about what to do.
9. E9 — wise_man_advises_test: wise councilman advises the apple vs coin test (apple ? free; coin ? killed).
10. E10 — judge_offers_test: judge stretches out apple and coin to the boy.
11. E11 — boy_takes_apple: boy grabs the apple with a laugh.
12. E12 — boy_set_free: boy is set free without punishment.

Short Interpretation and Relation to the Paper
Support for the sixmotif taxonomy
The canonical Grimm run produces a reproducible sixway partition that maps directly to the manuscript’s motif taxonomy (BRE, APD, FM/EM, Other, DCO, GD). The motif cardinalities and representative permutations provide concrete, reproducible exemplars for interpretation.
Silhouette near zero
A silhouette value close to zero indicates soft boundaries in embedding space. This is expected: motifs capture structural tendencies rather than sharply separated geometric clusters. This supports using motifs for qualitative interpretation, exemplar selection, and reporting motif cardinalities.
Practical conclusion
The Grimm canonical run reinforces the paper’s central claim: the peripheral cloud consistently organizes into six structural motifs that illuminate patterns of narrative incoherence. The reproducible motif assignments and exemplars provide a stable foundation for figures, analyses, and discussion.
