# Agents and their actions
Ten maidens took their lamps.
(Agent action) They went to meet the bridegroom.

# Classification / state
Five of them were foolish.
Five of them were wise.

# Possessions and contrasts
The foolish took their lamps but took no oil with them.
The wise took flasks of oil with their lamps.

# Temporal / event
As the bridegroom was delayed, they all slumbered and slept.
At midnight there was a cry: “Behold, the bridegroom! Come out to meet him.”

# Reaction / preparation
Then all those maidens rose and trimmed their lamps.

# Request / speech acts
The foolish said to the wise, “Give us some of your oil, for our lamps are going out.”
The wise replied, “Perhaps there will not be enough for us and for you; go rather to the dealers and buy for yourselves.”

# Outcome / result
And while they went to buy, the bridegroom came.
Those who were ready went in with him to the marriage feast, and the door was shut.

# Aftermath / plea
Afterward the other maidens came also, saying, “Lord, lord, open to us.”
But he replied, "Truly, I say to you, I do not know you.”
